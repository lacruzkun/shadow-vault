## Introduction
This section is dedicated to reviewing how programming language interpreters are implemented and executed. To build an interpreter that is light weight, beginner friendly and has a rich type system even though it's an interpreter, we have to look at how existing interpreters have handled or struggled with this balance, because i believe types are an important aspect of programming. I will be looking into different language implementations across different paradigms such as Rust, Haskell, Lisp, Python, Lua, Ocaml and JavaScript Runtimes, I'll look at their implementation approaches, common challenges they raise, before discussing how these inform my own design.
